    Records tumbled in the Boxing Day sales today as frenetic shoppers sent tills into meltdown. Millions of bargain-hunters made a beeline for the high street, with some queuing since midnight to ensure they bagged the best deals. Retailers up and down the country slashed prices and opened as early as 6am in a bid to entice them to part with their cash. A record 10million shoppers are expected to have spent around £3billion - £4.8million a minute - by the end of the day. In London's West End shopping mecca, by 11am footfall was up 31.3 per cent on Boxing Day last year with sales fuelled by rich tourists. The UK average footfall was up by 21.6 per cent.

    Attractions ticket specialist AttractionTix.co.uk have announced the winners of their 2015 Worldwide Attractions Awards, the only awards solely dedicated to recognising excellence within the attractions industry. Over 13,500 public votes from travel and attraction fans were cast, resulting in the¬ top attractions across 15 different categories including the Best Attraction in the World, Teens' Choice and Most Romantic Attraction or Tour. The Coca-Cola London Eye won BEST ATTRACTION IN THE UK along with being highly commended in the BEST ATTRACTION IN LONDON category.

    Article 3

    Buckingham Palace have shared an adorable picture of the Duchess of Cambridge on Twitter, wishing her a happy birthday. Kate Middleton, who turns 34 today, can be seen grinning in the picture of her when she was a schoolgirl, next to a recent photo of her smiling. The picture was tweeted from the account @BritishMonarchy, alongside the message: "Wishing The Duchess of Cambridge @KensingtonRoyal a very happy birthday! #HappyBirthdayHRH" It is not know what she will be doing to mark the occasion, but it is thought she could celebrate with a quiet candlelit dinner with her husband, Prince William.

    Article 4

    When Camden Market first opened in 1973, it was based in a bomb-damaged packing warehouse and was only expected to last a summer. Forty years later, it’s grown from counter-culture ghetto to capitalist mecca, with young tourists flocking to buy everything from Doc Martins and designer furniture to vinyl records and bongs. Now though, stall holders say they are in a “state of fear” over plans to list the market on the stock exchange and plough the proceeds into developing 170 new flats, a potential boutique hotel, cinema and designer stores. Market veterans warn the plans risk sucking the “charm and creativity” out of an area whose edgy, bohemian vibe inspired the likes of The Clash, Amy Winehouse and Pete Doherty.
